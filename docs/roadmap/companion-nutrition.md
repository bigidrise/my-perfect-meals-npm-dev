# Companion Nutrition — Future Roadmap Idea

**Origin:** Suggested by a tester during beta. Flagged as a serious expansion opportunity.
**Status:** Do not build. Document and revisit after human platform stability.

---

## The Concept

A separate nutrition system for pets — specifically dogs and cats — built on top of the same adaptive nutrition architecture that powers MPM for humans.

The positioning is NOT "pet meals." It is:

**Companion Nutrition** — personalized, protocol-aware feeding guidance for your pet, powered by the same intelligence behind your own nutrition.

---

## Why It Fits

MPM already understands:
- Ingredients and macro breakdowns
- Protocol-based restrictions (allergies, sensitivities, health conditions)
- Portioning by body weight and activity level
- Adaptive generation (swap ingredients, meet targets)
- Safety flags and hard blocks

All of that logic translates surprisingly well to:
- Species-specific nutrition requirements (dog vs. cat vs. senior pet)
- Toxic ingredient enforcement (no onions/garlic/grapes/xylitol for dogs)
- Weight management and activity-adjusted feeding
- Allergy-sensitive pet food generation
- Ingredient safety scanning

---

## Why It's Commercially Real

The fresh pet food market is a validated subscription ecosystem. People emotionally spend on pets at near-parental levels. Brands like The Farmer's Dog, Nom Nom, Ollie, and vet nutrition lines all charge premium prices for exactly this kind of personalized approach.

MPM already has the UX, the generation engine, and the personalization framework.
The gap is veterinary-safe logic, species-specific guardrails, and legal disclaimer scaffolding.

---

## Potential Expansion Paths

| Phase | Scope |
|---|---|
| MVP | Dog meal builder — generate simple whole-food meals based on breed, weight, age, activity |
| V2 | Ingredient safety scanner for pet food labels |
| V3 | Cat support, senior/allergy-sensitive protocols |
| V4 | Vet partnership integration, biomarker-aware feeding |
| Business | Premium add-on, family tier extension, white-label for vet practices |

---

## Requirements Before Building

- Veterinary nutrition consultant review of all generation logic
- Complete toxic ingredient blocklist per species
- Strong legal disclaimers (not veterinary medical advice)
- Separate content model from human nutrition (no cross-contamination of protocols)
- App Store positioning that does not trigger Apple's health/medical review triggers for animals

---

## Signal This Matters

When testers start naturally suggesting a feature, it means they are mentally positioning the product as a platform — not just an app. "Companion Nutrition" coming from a beta user means MPM is already being perceived as a food intelligence system, not just a meal generator. That is the right perception to be building.
