# My Perfect Meals
## Applied Nutrition Practicum
### A Practical Curriculum Block for NASM / ISSA Nutrition Certification Programs

---

## Executive Summary

Nutrition certification programs are built on science — macronutrient theory, metabolic function, dietary guidelines, and clinical frameworks. That foundation is essential. But there is a gap between passing a certification exam and sitting across from a real client who has Type 2 diabetes, three food allergies, a family history of hypertension, and no idea what to eat for dinner.

That gap is where practitioners lose confidence — and where clients lose trust.

**My Perfect Meals (MPM)** is an AI-powered clinical nutrition platform designed to close that gap. This curriculum block proposes a one-week practical module that trains students to use MPM as their primary client-facing tool, building the hands-on competency and business readiness that theory alone cannot provide.

Students who complete this block will leave certification not just knowing nutrition — they will know how to *practice* it, and they will have a professional business tool ready to use with real clients on day one.

---

## The Problem This Solves

### What Certification Teaches
- Macronutrient ratios and caloric targets
- Dietary guidelines and food group frameworks
- Metabolic physiology and energy systems
- Special population theory (diabetes, oncology, cardiovascular, etc.)

### What Certification Does Not Teach
- How to build a personalized meal plan in real time during a client session
- How to adjust for multiple overlapping conditions simultaneously (e.g., diabetic + anti-inflammatory + high protein + nut allergy)
- How to document, track, and iterate client nutrition over time
- How to communicate nutrition plans to clients in plain language
- How to run a nutrition coaching business

The gap between certification and competence is real, it is wide, and it matters — especially when students are working with clients who have serious medical conditions.

## About My Perfect Meals

My Perfect Meals is a clinical-grade AI nutrition platform built around four core principles:

1. **Protocol-First Generation** — Every meal is built against the user's full medical and dietary profile before any food preference is considered. Allergies, conditions (diabetes, oncology support, anti-inflammatory protocols, GLP-1 therapy, and more), macro targets, and dietary identity are all enforced as non-negotiable guardrails — not suggestions.

2. **Macro Truth** — The system never invents macro values. If a value is unknown, it is shown as unknown. Students learn to work with integrity, not approximations.

3. **Safety Guard** — A two-layer allergy protection system that blocks unsafe ingredients both at prompt generation and again at output validation. Students practice with real clinical safety standards, not academic exercises.

4. **Creator Ecosystem** — Students have access to nine meal creation tools: Create a Dish, Chef's Kitchen, Snack Creator, Beverage Creator, Craving Creator, Fridge Rescue, Meal Planner, Recipe Scan, and Holiday Feast. Each tool teaches a different aspect of real-world nutrition coaching.

## Curriculum Structure

### Module Title
**Applied Nutrition Practicum: From Certification to Client-Ready**

### Duration
5 days (1 instructional week) — suitable as a standalone capstone block or as the practical component of an existing nutrition module.

### Format
- Lecture + demonstration: 1 hour per day
- Hands-on lab (individual): 1 hour per day
- Partner practicum (paired coach/client sessions): 1.5 hours per day
- Debrief and reflection: 30 minutes per day

### Prerequisites
Students should have completed or be completing the core nutrition science curriculum of the host certification program.

## Day-by-Day Curriculum

---

### Day 1 — Profile as Protocol: Building a Client from the Ground Up

**Objective:** Understand how a clinical nutrition profile is structured and why the order of information matters.

**Lecture Topics**
- The 4-Layer Constraint Hierarchy: Medical → Dietary Identity → Cultural Preference → Behavioral Preference
- Why medical context overrides preference (clinical safety is not negotiable)
- The difference between a dietary restriction, a medical condition, and a personal preference — and why conflating them causes harm
- Introduction to the MPM platform: navigating the dashboard, understanding meal creators, reading a meal card

**Lab Exercise**
Each student creates two client profiles in the app:
1. A straightforward healthy adult with a standard macro target
2. A complex client: Type 2 diabetic, nut allergy, anti-inflammatory protocol, pescatarian

Students generate one meal from each profile and compare the output — observing how the system adapts ingredient choices, macro balance, and portion sizing across the two profiles.

**Discussion**
What surprised you? What would you have missed if you had built these plans manually? What does the system enforce that you might have forgotten to ask about?

**Partner Pairing Setup**
Students are assigned partners. Each pair will use the app's built-in ProCare system: one student is designated Coach, one is designated Client. They will rotate roles across the week.

---

### Day 2 — Special Populations: Practicing What Certification Warns About

**Objective:** Build hands-on competency with the clinical populations that certifications describe in theory but rarely practice.

**Lecture Topics**
- Diabetes in a coaching context: what a certified nutrition coach can and cannot do; how MPM's Glucose Guard protocol helps
- Anti-inflammatory nutrition: what it means in practice, what foods to avoid, how protocols enforce it automatically
- GLP-1 and weight-loss pharmacotherapy clients: appetite suppression, protein prioritization, portioning considerations
- Oncology-adjacent clients: what the platform's oncology support context provides, hard-blocked ingredients, and why treatment claims are never made
- When to refer: recognizing the boundary between nutrition coaching and medical nutrition therapy

**Lab Exercise**
Students receive three written client scenarios (provided by the instructor). For each scenario, students must:
1. Identify all relevant conditions and dietary identities
2. Build the client profile in the app
3. Generate a 3-meal day plan
4. Flag any ingredient or macro concerns using the meal card's protocol tags
5. Write a 2-sentence plain-language summary they would say to this client out loud

**Partner Practicum**
Coach student conducts a simulated first session with their Client partner. The Client student plays the role of one of the day's three scenarios. The Coach student must ask intake questions, build the profile live, and generate a first meal recommendation in real time.

---

### Day 3 — The Full Day Plan: Macro Budgeting and Meal Sequencing

**Objective:** Move from single meals to full-day nutrition architecture — the way real client plans work.

**Lecture Topics**
- Nutrition Budget: how to track daily protein, starchy carbs, and fiber across meals
- Starch Guard: how the platform limits high-glycemic starch allocation and shifts to fibrous carbs
- Meal sequencing strategy: breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks — building a day that hits targets without repetition
- Shopping list integration: how a meal plan becomes a real-world grocery list
- Weekly planning: how Meal Planner works and how to use it for client accountability

**Lab Exercise**
Students build a complete 7-day meal plan for their complex client from Day 1 (diabetic + anti-inflammatory + pescatarian). They must:
1. Hit the client's macro targets each day
2. Stay within Starch Guard limits
3. Include breakfast, lunch, dinner, and one snack per day
4. Export the shopping list and review it for completeness

**Partner Practicum**
Coach presents the completed 7-day plan to their Client partner. Client reviews the plan and asks at least three questions as a real client would: "Can I swap this ingredient?" / "What if I don't like this food?" / "Is this enough food for me?" Coach must respond and make live adjustments in the app.

---

### Day 4 — Recipe Scan and Real-World Inspiration: Meeting Clients Where They Are

**Objective:** Learn to use client-submitted food ideas as raw material for protocol-compliant personalized meals.

**Lecture Topics**
- Why clients will not eat plans they did not choose: the importance of inspiration-driven meal building
- Recipe Scan: how to capture any food idea (photo, text, voice) and rebuild it around a client's profile
- The customize-before-generating workflow: servings, adaptation style, protein level, prep style, cuisine
- The preview-before-saving principle: nothing is committed until the coach and client agree it is right
- Craving Creator: how to honor a craving without abandoning clinical protocols
- Fridge Rescue: the practical tool for clients who say "I don't know what to eat with what I have"

**Lab Exercise**
Each student finds three food images or descriptions from any external source (social media, a menu, a cookbook photo). For each:
1. Capture it using Recipe Scan
2. Apply the settings appropriate for their complex client (from Day 1)
3. Review the preview — does it meet protocol?
4. Save or regenerate with one setting change and explain why

**Partner Practicum**
Client partner submits three meal ideas "from their week" (chosen freely by the student). Coach must use Recipe Scan to convert all three into client-appropriate meals, presenting the preview for each and getting client sign-off before saving.

**Discussion**
How does this change the conversation with a resistant client? What does "I hate healthy food" actually mean when you can start from what they already want to eat?

---

### Day 5 — Business Launch: Your Pro Code, Your Practice, Your Brand

**Objective:** Understand how to operate as a professional nutrition coach from day one of certification.

**Lecture Topics**
- The MPM ProCare system: how to manage a client roster, send tablet messages, assign meal plans, and track progress
- The Pro Portal: coach dashboard, client onboarding, communication tools
- Your Pro Code: what it is, how clients use it to join your practice, how it follows you regardless of where you work
- Marketing your practice: how to use your Pro Code as a credential and a conversion tool
- Business structure basics: pricing your services, free versus paid client tiers, building recurring relationships
- The 6-Month Post-Certification Launch Period (see below)

**Lab Exercise**
Students set up their full Pro profile: coach bio, specialty areas, onboard their partner as a formal client through the ProCare system, and send their first formal client check-in via Tablet.

**Capstone Partner Practicum**
Full simulated coaching session — 45 minutes:
- Client partner presents as a new client with a complete fictional history (provided on a card)
- Coach conducts intake, builds profile, generates a 3-day starter plan, walks the client through it, sends a summary via Tablet, and schedules a follow-up
- Instructor observes and evaluates against the rubric below

**Debrief and Graduation**
Each student receives their personal Pro Code. Discussion: What do you know now that you did not know at the start of this week? What client scenario still concerns you? What is your plan for the first 30 days after certification?

## Assessment Rubric

| Competency | Weight |
|---|---|
| Client profile accuracy — correctly captured conditions, restrictions, and targets | 20% |
| Protocol compliance — generated meals respect all clinical and dietary constraints | 20% |
| Macro accuracy — daily plans meet targets within acceptable range | 15% |
| Client communication — plain-language explanation of plan and adjustments | 20% |
| Live adaptability — adjusted plan in real time in response to client questions | 15% |
| Business readiness — Pro profile complete, client onboarded, Tablet message sent | 10% |

## The Post-Certification Launch Program

Completing this curriculum block entitles students to the following upon certification:

### 6 Months Free Pro Access
Students receive a 6-month complimentary subscription to My Perfect Meals Pro following certification. This covers:
- Full client roster management
- All nine meal creation tools
- ProCare communication suite (Tablet messaging, check-ins, notes)
- Meal planning and shopping list tools
- Access to all clinical protocols

This period is designed to give graduates time to build a client base, establish a pricing model, and develop confidence before their first billing cycle begins.

### Personal Pro Code — Yours Forever
Every graduate receives a unique Pro Code that:
- Connects clients directly to their practice inside the app
- Travels with them regardless of employer, gym, studio, or platform
- Functions as a verifiable professional credential in marketing materials
- Tracks client enrollment and engagement over time

The Pro Code is permanent. A coach who graduates with it retains it for the life of their practice.

### Student-to-Student Practice Network
While enrolled, students can pair with classmates inside the app — one as coach, one as client — allowing real-time supervised practice using actual platform tools. This is not a simulation. It is real coaching work on a live platform, with real data, real protocol enforcement, and real feedback.
