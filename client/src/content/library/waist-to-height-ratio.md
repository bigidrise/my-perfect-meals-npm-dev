# Understanding Waist-to-Height Ratio

Waist circumference is one of the most important indicators of metabolic health.

While Body Mass Index (BMI) estimates weight relative to height, it does not measure fat distribution. Fat stored around the organs — called visceral fat — is strongly associated with cardiovascular disease, insulin resistance, and metabolic syndrome.

Researchers often use a simple rule known as the waist-to-height ratio. The guideline is that your waist should be less than half of your height.

For example:
If you are 70 inches tall, a waist under 35 inches is considered within the healthier range.

My Perfect Meals uses this measurement to improve nutrition precision. When waist measurements suggest increased visceral fat risk, the system may adjust macro recommendations to better support metabolic health.

This measurement does not diagnose medical conditions. It simply helps guide more personalized nutrition planning.
